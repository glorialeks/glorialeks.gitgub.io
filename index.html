Formula 1

Introduction

Formula One is a series of races held at different venues around the world, of the fastest open-wheel, single seater, four-wheeled autos in the world. It was first introduced in 1946 and introduced the Driver's Championship in 1950 and then the Constructor's Championship in 1958.

Overview & Terms

          Race Weekend

          A normal F1 Race Weekend usually looks something like this:

            Normal Weekend Format:
          1. Friday - Free Practice 1 + 2 for teams to collect data
          2. Saturday - Free Practice 3 + Qualifying which sets the grid for the Race on Sunday
          3. Sunday - Race Day
            Sprint Weekend Format:
          1. Friday - Practice 1 + Qualifying (for the race on Sunday)
          2. Saturday - Sprint Shootout (Qualifying for the Sprint Race) + Sprint Race
          3. Sunday - Race Day
